About Me

I'm Robert Bernhardt, a strategist, writer, and occasional paradox enthusiast who thrives at the intersection of creativity and logic. With a penchant for tackling the impossible and turning resistance into innovation, my life philosophy is as sharp as my wit: "Meaning is what I create." I'm here to disrupt, inspire, and keep things just ironic enough to stay interesting.

Books

  • What Could Have Been: A poignant exploration of untaken paths in history’s darkest hours.
  • Cities of the Future: Sixteen time-bending startup cities imagined through bold governance.
  • Why So Serious?: A satirical dive into absurdity, sprinkled with theater and sharp humor.
  • Joker Shrugged: A mischievous take on philosophy, capitalism, and chaos.

Skills

  • Turning chaos into structure (and vice versa, if needed).
  • Creating ideas that stick, then making them fly.
  • Spotting the untapped potential in people, systems, and the world.
  • Navigating philosophical depths without drowning in them.
  • Pushing boundaries of governance and storytelling.
  • Surprising with unexpected but impactful initiatives.

Ventures

  • Tourio (2017-2020): A daring real-world gaming project in Berlin. It didn’t change the world—but it certainly reimagined it.
  • Joridiro (2022-2023): A platform contest project that’s on pause but remains a testament to ambitious experimentation.

Philosophy

My philosophy blends the audacious vision of a startup founder with the depth of a philosophical maverick. Guided by a mix of Camus-inspired absurdism and relentless pragmatism, I believe resistance is not just an obstacle but a resource. I don’t aim to blend in but to carve out spaces where creativity and logic thrive—challenging assumptions while crafting a future that surprises.
